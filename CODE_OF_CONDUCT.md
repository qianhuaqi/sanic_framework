# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in LingShu a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, caste, sexual identity or orientation, or other protected or personal characteristics.

We act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that supports the community include:

- showing empathy and kindness;
- respecting different viewpoints, experiences, and technical backgrounds;
- giving and accepting constructive feedback;
- taking responsibility for mistakes and repairing harm;
- focusing on what is best for the project and its users;
- keeping technical disagreements evidence-based and proportionate;
- protecting private security reports, credentials, personal data, and confidential information.

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- sexualized language, imagery, or attention;
- insulting, demeaning, discriminatory, or inflammatory comments;
- harassment in public or private channels;
- threats, stalking, intimidation, or sustained disruption;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- deliberately exposing confidential vulnerability details or credentials;
- manipulating reviews, impersonating contributors, or misrepresenting authorship;
- retaliation against a person who raises a good-faith concern;
- other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository Issues, Pull Requests, reviews, Discussions, release channels, meetings, chat spaces, events, and private communications made in an official project role. It also applies when a person publicly represents LingShu.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the project maintainers. Use GitHub Private Vulnerability Reporting when the concern also involves a security vulnerability. When no private repository channel is available, use a private contact channel listed on the maintainer's GitHub profile.

Do not post sensitive personal details, vulnerability evidence, or private correspondence in a public Issue.

Reports are handled as confidentially as practical. Maintainers will avoid conflicts of interest; a maintainer named in a report must not control the investigation or outcome.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take actions including:

1. **Correction** — private clarification or warning for inappropriate behavior.
2. **Warning** — formal notice with conditions for continued participation.
3. **Temporary restriction** — time-limited restriction from project interaction.
4. **Permanent restriction** — permanent removal from project spaces or roles.

The response will consider intent, impact, repetition, power imbalance, remediation, risk to others, and the need to protect reporters. Bad-faith or knowingly false reports may also violate this policy; an unproven good-faith report does not.
